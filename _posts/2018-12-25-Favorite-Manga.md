You may not know this about me, but I'm kind of a weeb. Its pretty shameful actually, especially since I look down on weebs, but my stuggles my weebery is not the the point of this post. As my time as a closet weeb I read a great number of manga from a variety of genre, some were good and others not so much. Like everyone I slowly built up a list of manga that I liked; my list contains many of the same manga that that others have on their lists: Berserk, Full Metal Alchemist, 20th Century Boys, and Grand Blue, a list composed of mostly fantasy/sci-fi and comedy. But of all the manga that I have read All-Rounder Meguru (ARM) is my favorite. It's different from the other manga on the list, as it falls into the slice of life genre.

# The Author

To understand ARM you have to understand the author, Hiroki Endo, and his first work "Eden: Its an Endless World." To learn more about Endo one would turn to his Wikipedia page, but all they would find are a list of his works, when he was born (Nov 3, 1970), and his alma mater (Musashino Art University). To really understand his personality the best places to look (at least for someone who only reads english) are his twitter feeds, short stories, and "exerpts" from ends of chapters.
